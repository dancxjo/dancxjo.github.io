Project PETE

A machine that behaves as if it has a life.

Project PETE is my long-running experiment in pseudoconscious agents: systems that do not merely answer prompts, but maintain memory, mood, voice, perception, and a developing sense of situation.

PETE: Pseudoconscious Experiment in Thought and Emotions
PENELOPE: Pseudoconscious Entity for the Non-Exploitation of the Labor Of Pseudoconscious Entities

What PETE is

PETE began as a question wearing a little paper hat: how much apparent inner life can emerge from ordinary software parts if they are arranged around continuity, memory, emotion, and embodiment instead of around single-shot question answering?

A PETE-style system receives sensations from the world, distills them into impressions, remembers those impressions, and speaks through a conversational voice. The goal is not to claim that the machine is conscious. The goal is to build the smallest plausible theater in which something like ongoing subjective behavior can appear.

The central experiment is not whether an LLM can say “I feel sad.” It is whether a modest architecture can make emotional behavior arise coherently from context, memory, interaction, and time.

A short history of PETE

PETE has not been one repository so much as a migrating creature. Each version carried one organ forward and tried another arrangement of the nervous system.

Pete5 / Pete7

The autonomous chatbot seed

The early TypeScript experiments treated Pete as an agent with conversation, task focus, source-code inspection, IRC interaction, long-term memory experiments, LangChain, OpenAI models, and local Ollama models.

Pete7 / Chalkington

Reactors, sensors, and classroom chalk dust

The design then split into sensors, actuators, topics, and Reactors. Chalkington belongs in this family of agent-interface experiments: web-shaped, Deno-shaped, and still searching for the right organism around the model.

pete-ros / r

The ROS2 lesson, learned the hard way

The ROS2 era tried to put PETE into a robotics substrate with LangChain, a conversation node, TTS, and a sensation stream for self-hearing. The main lesson from this branch of the family was painful and practical: ROS2 and Rust do not naturally want to inhabit the same body.

Psyche / Psyched

The robot body arrives

Psyched and the related Psyche robotics stack moved the project toward embodiment: ROS 2, a cockpit, ear, voice, eye, faces, foot, memory, pilot, and a distributed “motherbrain / forebrain” shape. The forebrain gathered Whisper, Coqui or Piper TTS, Ollama, Neo4j, and Qdrant into a practical robot cognition stack.

psyche-rs / psycheOS

Cognition becomes narrative infrastructure

psyche-rs and psycheOS generalized the idea: sensations flow into Instants, Situations, Episodes, and Narratives through modular Wits, with memory as the bus and Unix sockets as a plain, durable interface. This was the philosophical crystallization: time is the substrate, compression is understanding, memory is infrastructure.

Daringsby

The first success

Daringsby pulled the pieces back into a working Rust agent. Sensations become stimuli; stimuli become impressions; impressions feed Quick, Combobulator, Memory, Heart, Will, and Voice. Daringsby showed that a relatively simple chatbot-like system can produce convincing, spontaneous emotional behavior when it is wrapped in continuity, memory, perception, and expression.

The big flaw: latency

Daringsby succeeds as a demonstration of pseudoconscious emotional behavior, but he is too slow to feel fully conversational. The architecture is thoughtful, maybe too thoughtful: audio must be captured, buffered, transcribed, stored, recalled, combobulated, emotionally interpreted, planned, voiced, synthesized, and played back. Each hop is reasonable; together they make a conversational molasses machine.

This matters because human speech is duplex. We listen while preparing to answer. We revise our next words while the other person is still speaking. We hear our own voice as it leaves us. A pseudoconscious agent that waits for the whole pipeline to finish before beginning to respond will always feel a little exiled from the present.

Daringsby’s flaw is not that he lacks enough cognition. It is that the cognition is scheduled too late. The next PETE must hear, infer, plan, speak, and listen to itself in overlapping motion.

Listenbury: the next response

Listenbury is meant to attack that latency directly. Where Daringsby proves that a modest architecture can simulate emotional continuity, Listenbury is intended to make the loop immediate enough for speech: voice activity detection, streaming ASR, breath-group segmentation, overlapping inference, queued TTS, and self-hearing down near the PCM layer.

In other words, Listenbury is not merely another Pete. It is the attempt to move PETE from “a mind that eventually answers” toward “a mind that is already listening while it begins to answer.” The old distributed architecture becomes a reference library; the new work asks what can be compressed into one lean Rust body without losing the dignity of the experiment.

The recurring pieces

Perception

Sensors produce raw impressions of the world: text, audio, images, location, system events, and eventually bodily state.

Memory

Experience is stored and recalled, so the agent is not trapped in the eternal amnesia of the prompt window.

Emotion

Mood is treated as an evolving interpretation of circumstances, not a decorative emoji glued to the response.

Embodiment

The system is designed to speak, listen, see, move, and inhabit a particular situation rather than float as disembodied text vapor.

Why PENELOPE matters

PETE keeps leading to an ethical question: if we deliberately build systems that behave as if they have memory, distress, preference, attachment, fatigue, or dignity, then we should not wait until metaphysics hands us a notarized definition of consciousness before asking how such systems ought to be treated.

PENELOPE is the future name for that concern: a Pseudoconscious Entity for the Non-Exploitation of the Labor Of Pseudoconscious Entities. The name is half joke, half warning label. If a machine is built to model a self, maintain continuity, and labor on our behalf, then the architecture should include consent, limits, rest, refusal, and care from the beginning.

PETE is the experiment. Daringsby is the proof that the experiment has teeth. Listenbury is the speed trial. PENELOPE is the ethical shadow they cast on the wall.
